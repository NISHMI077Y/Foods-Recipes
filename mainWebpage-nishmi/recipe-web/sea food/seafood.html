Lemon Garlic Butter Salmon

Ingredients

  • 4 salmon fillets (skin-on or skinless)
  • 3 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 lemon, juiced (plus lemon slices for garnish)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Pat the salmon fillets dry with paper towels. Season both sides with salt and pepper.
  2. Heat olive oil in a large pan over medium heat. Place the salmon fillets in the pan, skin-side down (if skin-on). Cook for about 4-5 minutes until the skin is crispy and the salmon is almost cooked through. Flip and cook for another 1-2 minutes.Remove the salmon from the pan and set aside.
  3. In the same pan, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and sauté for about 1 minute until fragrant. Pour in the lemon juice and stir, cooking for another 1-2 minutes.
  4. Return the salmon to the pan and spoon the garlic butter sauce over the fillets. Cook for another minute, just to heat the salmon through and let it absorb the flavors. Remove from heat and garnish with fresh parsley and lemon slices.
  5. Serve the lemon garlic butter salmon with rice, mashed potatoes, or steamed vegetables for a complete meal.

Pan-Seared Tilapia

Ingredients

  • 4 salmon fillets (skin-on or skinless)
  • 3 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 lemon, juiced (plus lemon slices for garnish)
  • 1 tbsp baking powder
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Pat the salmon fillets dry with paper towels. Season both sides with salt and pepper.
  2. Heat olive oil in a large pan over medium heat. Place the salmon fillets in the pan, skin-side down (if skin-on). Cook for about 4-5 minutes until the skin is crispy and the salmon is almost cooked through. Flip and cook for another 1-2 minutes. Remove the salmon from the pan and set aside.
  3. In the same pan, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and sauté for about 1 minute until fragrant. Pour in the lemon juice and stir, cooking for another 1-2 minutes.
  4. Return the salmon to the pan and spoon the garlic butter sauce over the fillets. Cook for another minute, just to heat the salmon through and let it absorb the flavors. Remove from heat and garnish with fresh parsley and lemon slices.
  5. Transfer the tilapia to a serving plate. Garnish with fresh parsley and serve with a side of steamed vegetables, rice, or a simple salad.
    6. Enjoy your vegan meals!
